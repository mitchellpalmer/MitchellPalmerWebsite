Introduction

I am passionate about economics, politics, and technology. In particular, I believe in the power of private enterprise, decentralised decision-making, and stable institutions to deliver prosperity and freedom.

My experience ranges from policy analysis to software development to content production. In politics, I pride myself on building force-multiplier software, which allows small teams to achieve big results.

I am looking for opportunities in the commercial sector to apply my analytical and technical skills in new industries. I am also available to assist not-for-profits, advocacy groups, and political parties looking to achieve change. I am based in Singapore, but I do not work on partisan political activity here. I was raised in New Zealand and am available to work remotely.

Experience

Political Technology Consultant

September 2017 — present (excluding periods of full-time employment with the Parliamentary Service)

Since the 2017 general election, I have worked as a political technology consultant for a variety of causes. In particular, I have worked for ACT New Zealand for a sustained period and have been responsible for:

  • Leading the Party's migration to the NationBuilder platform
  • Redeveloping the ACT website after a rebranding of the Party,
  • developing, from scratch, a new mission-critical electronic electoral roll management system, during the 2017 election,
  • creating election result analytics using GIS software and R, and
  • building bespoke digital campaigns, often with tight turnaround times, including complex Angular-based apps which extended the functionality available in NationBuilder.

Digital Media Specialist — ACT Leader's Office

Parliamentary Service | November 2017 - August 2020 (including other positions)

I was employed full-time in the parliamentary office of David Seymour MP, the Leader of ACT, New Zealand’s second-largest opposition party. I had responsibility for the development of economic policy, including the Party’s Alternative Budget. In the development of this policy, which is now a central plank of the Party’s election campaign, I worked with renowned economists and fiscal policy experts from across New Zealand. I was also responsible for the maintenance of the party’s website, the authoring of mass emails on David’s behalf, and the production of live video, a role which took on special importance during the COVID-19 pandemic, as well as liaising with external service providers, including printing companies and advertising agencies.

In my previous role in the Parliamentary Service, I worked on a casual basis as Parliamentary Member Support to David Seymour MP in his constituency office in Epsom. I assisted with running events, organising direct mail deliveries, and coordinating access to the electoral roll.

Board Member — Auckland Grammar School

Late 2018 - late 2019

I was the first democratically-elected Student Representative on Auckland Grammar's Board of Trustees. As a fully-empowered board member under the Education Act, I was responsible for the governance of this $40-million-turnover, 2500-student Crown Entity. I was involved in the financial planning for the school's 150th anniversary and a $12 million major capital project. I was also a member of the Education Committee during the School's transition away from Cambridge International Examinations in Year 11 and its development of a new in-house certificate.

Auckland Grammar School

January 2015 - December 2019 — CIE A-Levels and NZQA Scholarship

I attended Auckland Grammar School, New Zealand's pre-eminent public boys' school, as a boarder on the Cooper-Connell Scholarship.

While there, I studied for Cambridge International A-Levels in Economics (A*), History (A*), Maths (A), and Physics (A). In addition to my Cambridge course, I also sat the NZ Scholarship exams, the most prestigious examinations offered by New Zealand's national qualifications authority. I received a Scholarship in Economics three times (first in Form 5/Year 11, being the youngest Grammar student ever to win an Economics Scholarship), two Outstanding Scholarships in History, one Scholarship and one Outstanding Scholarship in English, two Scholarships in Physical Education, a Scholarship in Media Studies, a Scholarship in Agricultural and Horticultural Science, and a Scholarship in Statistics.

I also performed strongly in economics competitions. I was the first-placed student in the 2019 New Zealand Economics Olympiad. I was also National Champion of the New Zealand Economics Competition, in Divison 1 in 2019 and Division 2 in 2018. I was also Governor of the Grammar team who were runners-up in the 2019 Monetary Policy Challenge run by the Reserve Bank. I was also a finalist in the 2019 Richard Koch Breakthrough Prize, an international all-ages essay competition run by the Institute of Economic Affairs in London.

I was also involved in the extra-curricular life of the school. In 2019, I was a school and Tibbs House prefect. I was also a committee member of the Auckland Grammar Investment Club and the founder of the Forum, a student debating society. I also played cricket, disc ultimate, and squash socially and was a referee for the Auckland Rugby Referees Association for 3 years.
